Kayama Shimeji Biography

History

Prior to the Tragedy


From an early age, Kayama grew up in Poland with her parents until her father went to Japan to work to support the family. Her mother was a artist and sold paintings, but the money was too small to cover basic needs.

While attending high school in her country, Kayama experienced unpleasant treatment from her classmates due to being a mixed race and quite clumsy. She was physically and emotionally abused by other people which always ended with Kayama being reprimanded. She is reluctant to talk about the situation where she was deliberately pushed down the stairs during a school break, suffering serious injuries that required her to have stitches. She decided to cover the scars with bandages, and subsequent motorcycle accidents added several new marks around her neck and arms. She also mentions how old school bullies never faced any consequences. This whole experience made Kayama distrustful and backward with social interactions, and the slightest taunt towards her caused her to become aggressive.

Kayama changed schools and moved to Japan. She was an ambitious child who liked to learn new things, which her parents quickly noticed. A year before joining Hope's Peak Academy, she met Mondo, with whom she quickly became friends. After collecting enough money, her parents decided to enroll her in Hope's Peak Academy for the Reserve Course, so that she could develop traditions in Japan, while meeting new people.

During her first trimester, Kayama didn't have many friends, so she spent all her time on self-development, which contributed to her passion for playing video games and riding bikes, gaining attention from other students and the management of Hope's Peak Academy, until she did not suffer a serious knee injury that prevented her from continuing to ride. After purchasing her first motorcycle, Kayama took part in tournaments, winning cups and medals. The director Jin Kirigiri, noticing her emerging talent, suggested that she move on to the Main Course. They wanted to encourage people to continue paying for the Reserve Course, presenting Kayama as a living example of a person who gained his talent "through this school". They allowed her to join Class 77-B before the end of her first trimester.

During the Tragedy


During the Tragedy, Kayama escaped from the Ultimate Despairs by riding her motorbike. One day there was an accident in which her vehicle was destroyed, so she had to walk even though she injured her knees. While outside, she used a baseball bat to defend herself against barbed wire, and a Monokuma mask to hide her identity. She successfully destroyed nearby robots and machines, much to Junko's annoyance. As there was a live broadcast of the Killing Game, Kayama often watched the event in which Mondo also participated.

When one of the Ultimate Despair tried to catch her, she hide in a tight alley where Kazuichi happened to be. She bump into him and beg him to spare her life in exchange for helping him work on the machines. Kazuichi was unconvinced at first, but eventually agreed to the deal, hiding her from the other Ultimate Despairs in his workshop.

Kayama often helped Kazuichi and had her own duties such as getting some scrap metal and parts for his machines. One day, while she was routinely on the surface looking for additional components for the robots, she came across the students who had survived Junko's first Killing Game. She decided to join the Future Foundation with them, creating a plan to capture the Remnants of Despair.

Side: Hope - The Academy of Hope and Students of Despair

Kayama returned in Side: Hope alongside the rest of the Class 77-B, who had been resurrected by Izuru Kamukura although Izuru was now combined with the personality and memories of Hajime. Kayama and her classmates arrived at the island where the Future Foundation Headquarters were situated, having left from Jabberwock Island and proceeded by rescuing Byakuya Togami and Yasuhiro Hagakure from being trapped under rubble, they then began their fight against Ryota's brainwashed rescue squad troops, including the Super High School Level Elite Task Force.

Kayama appears with jet roller skates, which Kazuichi probably created so she could move faster anywhere. Holding a baseball bat stripped of barbed wire, she stuns most of the Super High School Level Elite Task Force of Future Foundation shooters to aid Hajime Hinata attempt to reach the broadcasting tower where Ryota was situated.

Later on, Kayama helped convince Ryota to abandon his plan to brainwash the world. When Kazuichi mentions that Hajime is from the Reserve Course, Kayama adds that she was barely present at all and they still consider her to be part of their class.

As everyone begins to return to the boat, Kayama is the last to pass Makoto Naegi. When he asks if she's sure she wants to go back to Jabberwock Island, she hesitates for a moment, but finally states that they need a guardian from the Future Foundation who will monitor them. She also says that she can't just abandon her classmates like that.

Kazuichi is approached by Kayama, who thanks him for the jet roller skates that came in handy during the fight. Kazuichi states that this is not a problem for him when suddenly Kayama kisses him on the cheek, which surprises him and his face turns red. Additionally, Sonia states that they would make a good duo, to which Kayama also blushes. He then jokingly called her "Miss Kayama", which caught her off guard.
