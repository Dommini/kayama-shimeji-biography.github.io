History

Prior to the Tragedy



From an early age, Kayama grew up in Poland with her parents until her father went to Japan to work to support the family. Her mother was an artist and sold paintings, but the money was too small to cover basic needs.

While attending high school in her country, Kayama experienced bullying from her classmates due to being a mixed race and quite clumsy. She was physically and emotionally abused by other people which always ended with Kayama being reprimanded. She was reluctant to talk about the situation where she had been deliberately pushed down the stairs during a school break, suffering serious injuries that required her to have stitches. She decided to cover the scars with bandages. She gained more marks on her neck and arms due subsequent motorcycle accidents. She also mentions how old school bullies never faced any consequences. This whole experience made Kayama distrustful and reserved with social interactions. The slightest taunt towards her makes her act aggressive.

Kayama changed schools and moved to Japan. She was an ambitious child who liked to learn new things, which her parents quickly noticed. A year before joining Hope's Peak Academy, she met Mondo, and surprisingly quickly befriend him. After saving up enough, her parents decided to enroll her in Hope's Peak Academy for the Reserve Course, so that she could learn Japanese traditions, while meeting new people.

During her first year, Kayama didn't have many friends, so she spent all her time on self-development, which contributed to her passion for playing video games and riding bikes, she gained attention of other students and the management of Hope's Peak Academy, until she did not suffer a serious knee injury that prevented her from continuing to ride. After purchasing her first motorcycle, Kayama took part in tournaments, winning cups and medals. The director Jin Kirigiri, noticing her emerging talent, suggested she would move on to the Main Course. They wanted to encourage people to continue paying for the Reserve Course, presenting Kayama as a living example of a person who gained their talent "thanks to this school". They allowed her to join Class 77-B before the end of her first year.



Danganronpa 3: The End of Hope's Peak High School - Despair Arc



Episode 02 - My Impurest Heart for You
A few weeks after Chisa Yukizome started working as a teacher, Kayama first appears at the door of Class 1-B during Akane's training with Nekomaru, which initially surprises the others. She explains that she is a new student from the Reserve Course and will be taking this class, remaining on the Main Course as the Ultimate Car Racer. Chisa enthusiastically welcomes her new student while the other students are skeptical about the new person. They feel a bit curious about how she managed to gain her talent, despite being previously in the Reserve Course.


While the others were playing video games, Kayama stood by and watched everyone else having fun. She was nervous to go up and ask if she could join, so she kept herself slightly distanced, watching Kazuichi and listening to him brag about the screen he had built.

When it turned out that Hiyoko had added aphrodisiac into the soup, Kayama couldn't resist the temptation to praise and interact with Kazuichi. Her stress was temporarily relieved by the soup's side effect.


Episode 03 - A Farewell to All Futures
At the beginning of the episode, Kayama and her classmates walked past Hajime heading toward the Main Course building. Later that day, Kayama was with her classmates during the break time in the school's park. She was listening to music on headphones at the empty table, staring at the others.

After a while, her MP3 breaks, making her complain loudly. It catches Ibuki's attention, so she called Kazuichi and asked him to take a look at the device. Wanting to impress Sonia, Kazuichi started bragging about his knowledge by quickly repairing her MP3. Kayama was surprised by his skills and smiled shyly, thanking him.


Episode 04 - The Melancholy, Surprise, and Disappearance of Nagito Komaeda
Kayama sits at a desk with the other students in the classroom, worrying about tomorrow's practice exams. Near the end, she learns that Chisa Yukizome's position has been changed for the Reserve Course, which makes Kayama frown in concern.


Episode 05 - The Beginning of the End
When Chisa Yukizome transferred to the Reserve Course, the class's attendance began to decline because of Kayama. She believed that games and fun were a waste of time, since she preferred to work on developing her talent, so she often missed the school days. When Chisa returned, she quickly noticed that someone (Kayama) was missing, along with Hiyoko, who later appeared in the doorway, surprising her with her more mature appearance.

Meanwhile, as Kayama was riding her motorbike back home, the wallet bomb that Junko left behind after getting out of the car explodes in front of her eyes, pushing her away several meters. The whole incident was noticed by Mondo, who helped her get up and back to normal. Unfortunately for Kayama, she suffered serious bruises and scars.


Episode 06 - A Despairfully Fateful Encounter
While the rest of the class was moving to the new school building, Kayama, wrapped in bandages, tries to recover from the accident. Kayama stated that she couldn’t join her classmates in this state. After a while, Chisa gave her a visit to check how she’s doing, wanting to find out the reasons for her absence, but Kayama assures her that she will return to her class once she recovers.


Episode 07 - The Biggest, Most Atrocious Incident in Hope's Peak Academy's History
Kayama receives a message sent by Junko regarded the truth about the Reserve Course and The Tragedy of Hope's Peak Academy, which shocked her so much that for a split second she had prophetic visions in her head that portrayed something bad. Despite her injuries, she goes to the new school building. She tried to push through the crowd to get into the building, but unfortunately she’s quickly pushed by the rest of the outraged students, falling to the floor and lost consciousness.


Episode 08 - The Worst Reunion by Chance
Nagito, wanting to return to Hope's Peak Academy, notices Kayama lying in the crowd, so he decides to push through the remaining students, taking her to the school nurse's room without any issues. Seeing her unconscious state, he hopes she will be okay, noticing potential in her that he cannot describe, before leaving the room to greet the class after a long absence.


Episode 10 - Smile at Hope in the Name of Despair
Finally, Kayama wakes up from unconsciousness and as she slowly gets up with a headache, she notices the students lying in front of the school gate. After a while, she suffers from vision attacks once again, but forces herself to leave the room and exit the school, thinking it might be dangerous to be here. When she reaches home, she falls on her bed, her visions haunting her. Falling into a deep sleep, she experiences another prophetic dream, telling about a great, despairful tragedy.


Episode 11 - Goodbye, Hope's Peak Academy
When their final lesson before graduation takes place, Kayama wakes up late, even though she did everything to prevent it. Before Chisa begins her speech, she notices that Kayama is missing, and then appeals to the others to find her after the meeting ends. Meanwhile, while riding her motorbike, Kayama notices dark clouds that worry her. When she gets there, something in the building explodes suddenly, and Kayama, seeing it, quickly retreated from the scene in fear.

At the end of the episode, when the deeds of the Remnants of Despair are presented, Kayama seeks shelter, stealing a Monokuma mask from the street and a holding baseball bat to defend himself.



During the Tragedy



During the Tragedy, Kayama escaped from the Ultimate Despairs by riding her motorbike. One day there she had an accident in which her vehicle was destroyed, so she had to walk even though she injured her knees. While outside, she used a baseball bat to defend herself against barbed wire, and a Monokuma mask to hide her identity. She successfully destroyed nearby robots and machines, much to Junko's annoyance. As there was a live broadcast of the Killing Game, Kayama often watched the event in which Mondo also participated.

When one of the Ultimate Despairs tried to catch her, she hid in a small alley where Kazuichi happened to be. She bumps into him and beg him to spare her life in exchange for helping him work on the machines. Kazuichi was unconvinced at first, but eventually agreed to the deal, hiding her from the other Ultimate Despairs in his workshop.

Kayama often helped Kazuichi and had her own duties such as getting some scrap metal and parts for his machines. One day, while she was routinely on the surface looking for additional components for the robots, she came across the students who had survived Junko's first Killing Game. She decided to join the Future Foundation with them, creating a plan to capture the Remnants of Despair

Real-life
After Nagito awoke from the simulation, he learned that all of his friends, including Kayama, were alive and awake from the Neo World Program. Nagito got into the boat with the rest of Class 77-B and they all then headed for the Future Foundation Headquarters.



Side: Hope - The Academy of Hope and Students of Despair


Kayama returned in Side: Hope alongside the rest of the Class 77-B, who had been resurrected by Izuru Kamukura, now combined with the personality and memories of Hajime. Kayama and her classmates arrived at the island where the Future Foundation Headquarters was situated, left from Jabberwock Island and proceeded by to rescue Byakuya Togami and Yasuhiro Hagakure from being trapped under rubble. They began fighting against Ryota's brainwashed rescue squad troops, including the Super High School Level Elite Task Force.

Kayama appeared with jet roller skates, which Kazuichi probably created so she could moved faster anywhere. Holding a baseball bat without barbed wire, she stunned most of the Super High School Level Elite Task Force of Future Foundation shooters to aid Hajime Hinata attempted to reach the broadcasting tower where Ryota was situated. Later on, Kayama helped convince Ryota to abandon his plan to brainwash the world. When Kazuichi mentions that Hajime is from the Reserve Course, Kayama added that she was barely present at all and they still consider her to be part of their class. As everyone begins to return to the boat, Kayama is the last to passed Makoto Naegi. When he asked if she's sure she wanted to go back to Jabberwock Island, she hesitated for a moment, but finally stated that they needed a guardian from the Future Foundation who would monitor them. She also said that she can't just abandon her classmates like that.

Kazuichi is approached by Kayama, who thanked him for the jet roller skates that came in handy during the fight. Kazuichi stated that this was not a problem for him when suddenly Kayama kissed him on the cheek, which surprised him and his face turned red. Additionally, Sonia stated that they would made a good duo, to which Kayama also blushed. He then jokingly called her "Miss Kayama", which caught her off guard.
